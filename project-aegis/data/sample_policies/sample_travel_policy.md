# Corporate Travel Policy

**Document ID:** TRV-POL-2026-V3  
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Policy Owner:** GCT-RM  
**Version:** V3 (supersedes V2 – 2024-01-01)

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs all business travel undertaken by employees on behalf of the
organisation. It sets out entitlements, booking procedures, reimbursable expenses,
and per-diem allowances applicable to both domestic and international travel.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all full-time employees, contractors, and consultants
travelling on approved company business.

---

## 3. Booking Procedure

### 3.1 Advance Booking

All travel must be booked a minimum of **7 calendar days** before the departure date
through the approved corporate travel portal. Emergency bookings within 7 days
require written approval from the employee's line manager.

### 3.2 Flight Class

| Journey Duration | Permitted Class |
| ---              | ---             |
| Under 5 hours    | Economy         |
| 5–8 hours        | Economy Plus    |
| Over 8 hours     | Business Class  |

### 3.3 Hotel Accommodation

Hotels must be selected from the preferred vendor list on the travel portal.
Where no preferred hotel is available, the nightly rate must not exceed:

| Region        | Maximum Nightly Rate |
| ---           | ---                  |
| Tier 1 Cities | $250 / £200 / €220   |
| Tier 2 Cities | $180 / £150 / €165   |
| Other         | $130 / £105 / €115   |

---

## 4. International Travel

### 4.1 Passport and Visa Requirements

Employees must hold a passport valid for at least **six months** beyond the
travel return date. Visa requirements must be verified at least 21 days before
departure. The company will reimburse visa application fees for approved trips.

### 4.2 Per Diem Allowances

Per diem covers meals and incidental expenses. Receipts are not required for
per diem claims, but per diem rates cannot be combined with individual meal claims.

| Country        | Daily Per Diem |
| ---            | ---            |
| USA            | $120           |
| United Kingdom | £95            |
| Germany        | €100           |
| Singapore      | SGD 160        |
| Japan          | ¥15,000        |
| Australia      | AUD 145        |
| UAE            | AED 380        |
| India          | ₹3,500         |

### 4.3 Ground Transportation

Employees may claim the following ground transportation expenses for approved
international business travel, provided original receipts are attached:

- Taxi and ride-sharing services (Uber, Lyft, Grab, Ola, etc.)
- Airport transfer coaches
- Metro and public transport passes for the duration of the trip
- Rental car (requires prior approval from line manager; economy category only)

Personal vehicle use for international travel is not reimbursable.

---

## 5. Domestic Travel

### 5.1 Mileage Reimbursement

Employees using personal vehicles for domestic business travel are reimbursed at
**₹7.00 per kilometre** (India) or **$0.67 per mile** (USA).

### 5.2 Local Ground Transportation

Taxi and ride-sharing expenses within the home country are reimbursable up to
a maximum of **$50 / £40 / €45** per day. Amounts exceeding this limit require
line manager approval.

---

## 6. Expense Submission

### 6.1 Submission Deadline

All travel expense claims must be submitted within **30 calendar days** of the
employee's return date. Claims submitted after 30 days will be rejected unless
an extension is approved by the Finance Controller.

### 6.2 Required Documentation

- Original receipts for all individual expenses above $25 / £20 / €22
- Boarding passes for all flights
- Hotel folios showing itemised charges
- Business purpose for each trip (enter in the travel portal)

### 6.3 Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are not reimbursable under any circumstances:

- Minibar and in-room movie charges
- Personal grooming and spa services
- Traffic fines and parking tickets
- Lost or stolen items
- Expenses for non-travelling family members or guests
- Upgrades purchased personally (only pre-approved upgrades are reimbursable)

---

## 7. Policy Violations

Repeated violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, including
withdrawal of travel booking privileges and recovery of incorrectly claimed amounts.
